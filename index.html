Peter Lik (born 1959) is an Australian photographer. He became a naturalized citizen of the United States in 2013.[2] Details of the deal have not been disclosed, but the company announced that a black-and-white version of "Ghost" shot in Antelope Canyon was purchased in 2014 for $6.5 million, making it the most expensive photo ever 。 Characterized by his style of expressing majestic nature using panoramic photography, he has been recognized by the American Professional Photographers Association (PPA), the Australian Professional Photographers Association (AIPP),In addition to being awarded the title of Master Photographer by Master Photographers International (MPIO), he received the Lifetime Achievement Award at the PPA's Imaging USA in 2015.

Career

Peter Rick was born in 1959 to Czech parents who emigrated to Melbourne, Australia after World War II.A Kodak brownie as a gift for his 8th birthday sparked his interest in photography, and he taught himself the technique.The first photo he took was of a spider web in his garden.
